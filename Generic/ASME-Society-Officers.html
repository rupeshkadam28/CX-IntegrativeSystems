President - Nominee

Richard T. Laudenat, P.E. (Fellow, ASME) will become the 138th president of The American Society of Mechanical Engineers (ASME) for the 2019-2020 term commencing in June of 2019. Mr. Laudenat has been an active member in ASME since 1991.

Laudenat has spent his career in the field of energy generation. In August 2015, he retired from GDF Suez (now ENGIE), a leading global energy operator, where he served as plant manager responsible for the operation of 30 electric generators at 10 plants throughout Connecticut. He was also responsible for strategic planning for generation assets; worked to develop plans and cost estimates for combustion turbine plant expansions; and was the project manager for the Waterbury Generation project, a 100 megawatt Simple Cycle Power Plant in Waterbury, Conn.

Prior to his work at GDF Suez, Laudenat held the position of director, Business, at Bartlett Group Holdings, Inc., responsible for developing new business opportunities within the commercial nuclear power sector. He also served as project manager at E.S. Boulos Co., one of the oldest, largest, and most experienced electrical and telecommunications contractors in the northeastern United States; and he worked for Northeast Utilities, a public utilities holding company, where he led business development for nuclear power service and cogeneration plant development.

Among his leadership positions at ASME, Laudenat served as a member of the Board of Governors; vice chair of the Sector Management Committee; senior vice president, Knowledge and Communities; and chair of the Council on Engineering Finance Committee. He also held several leadership roles in ASMEâ€™s energy initiatives, including vice president, Energy Conversion; chair of the Nuclear Engineering Division; and served on the ASME Nuclear Engineering Executive Committee; ASMEâ€™s Nuclear Heat Exchanger Committee; and the Electric Utilities Committee of the ASME Gas Turbine Division. In 2010, he was named a recipient of the ASME Dedicated Service Award.

Laudenat received both his master's degree in mechanical engineering and his MBA in management from the Hartford Graduate Center.

Board of Governors - Nominee

Todd R. Allen (Member, ASME) is the president and founder of Allen Research, Technologies and Services, Inc. (ARTS, Inc.), a professional services and consulting firm since 1993, focused on business solutions and strategy, new product development and leadership coaching.

Mr. Allen has a 39-year engineering career in the industries of petroleum, chemical, nonwovens, paper, consumer and personal care products, medical devices, pharmaceuticals and building products. He worked with Exxon Company USA, Shell Oil Company, Kimberly-Clark Corporation, Johnson & Johnson and Georgia-Pacific Corporation. He managed over 500 engineers and other professionals in over 30 countries. He has several inventions, including the Engineered Bellows Pump, the worldâ€™s most accurate disposable pump. He led projects for new factories and research facilities, and production machine lines, and launched several new global products.

Mr. Allen, an ASME member since 1979, began volunteering in 1995. He is the 2018 recipient of the Henry Laurence Gantt Medal for distinguished management and service achievement. He served on the Board of Diversity and Outreach, the Center for Leadership and Diversity, the Diversity & Inclusion Strategy Committee (past Chair) and the VOLT Academy. He helped ASME establish the Johnson & Johnson Consumer Companies diversity award and advised over 15 engineering societies in diversity initiatives.

Mr. Allen served 6 years on the Corporate & Foundation Alliance, partnering with the National Science Foundation (NSF) to increase under-represented students in STEM careers. He was a NSF grant co-principal investor and a National Academy of Engineering co-author. He holds certifications in Process Excellence 6-sigma, in Human Resources from Rutgers University and in Executive Leadership Coaching from the Hudson Institute of Santa Barbara.

Mr. Allen received his Master of Engineering Management from Syracuse University, his Master of Mechanical Engineering from Tulane University, and his Bachelor of Mechanical Engineering from the Georgia Institute of Technology.

Thomas R. Kurfess, Ph.D., P.E., (Fellow, ASME) is the HUSCO/Ramirez Distinguished Chair in Fluid Power and Motion Control and Professor in Mechanical Engineering at Georgia Tech. During 2012-2013 he was on leave serving as the Assistant Director for Advanced Manufacturing at the Office of Science and Technology Policy in the Executive Office of the President of the United States of America.
